# ADR-003: Regulatory Disclaimers and Compliance

**Status**: Accepted
**Date**: 2026-03-13
**Deciders**: Yuri Gui

## Context

Claude-VC produces financial analysis, valuation estimates, and investment recommendations. This creates regulatory risk under securities laws in multiple jurisdictions:

- **Canada**: Ontario Securities Act, CSA regulations. Providing investment advice requires registration as a Portfolio Manager or Investment Fund Manager. Exempt Market Dealer registration for certain dealing activities.
- **United States**: SEC regulations. Investment Adviser registration under the Investment Advisers Act of 1940.
- **General**: Any tool that appears to give personalized investment advice could be construed as acting as an unregistered adviser.

## Decision

### Disclaimer Framework

Every skill output that contains financial analysis, valuations, or investment recommendations must include a disclaimer. The disclaimers are tiered by output type:

**Standard Disclaimer** (appended to all `/vc` outputs):

```
---
DISCLAIMER: This analysis is generated by an AI tool for informational and
educational purposes only. It does not constitute investment advice, a
recommendation to buy or sell securities, or an offer to invest. All
financial projections and valuations are estimates based on limited
information and should not be relied upon for investment decisions. Consult
a qualified financial advisor and legal counsel before making any
investment. The creators of this tool are not registered as investment
advisers, broker-dealers, or exempt market dealers in any jurisdiction.
---
```

**Enhanced Disclaimer** (for valuation and terms analysis outputs):

```
---
DISCLAIMER: This valuation/terms analysis is a mathematical exercise using
publicly available methodologies. It is not an appraisal, fairness opinion,
or professional valuation. Actual company valuations depend on factors not
captured in this analysis. This tool does not provide legal advice regarding
term sheet provisions. Engage qualified legal and financial professionals
for all investment and securities matters.

Ontario/Canada users: This tool does not provide advice within the meaning
of the Securities Act (Ontario) or National Instrument 31-103. No
registration exemption is relied upon.
---
```

### Implementation

1. **Disclaimers in reference files**: A `references/disclaimers.md` file contains all disclaimer templates. Sub-skills load the appropriate disclaimer variant.

2. **Mandatory inclusion**: The orchestrator SKILL.md explicitly instructs Claude to append the relevant disclaimer to every output. This is a hard requirement, not optional.

3. **No personalized advice**: All skill instructions explicitly state that outputs are "general analysis" and "not tailored to any individual's investment objectives, financial situation, or risk tolerance."

4. **Configurable jurisdiction**: Users can set their jurisdiction in an optional config to get jurisdiction-specific disclaimer language. Default includes both Canadian and US language.

5. **No regulated activities**: The tool explicitly does not:
   - Recommend specific securities to purchase or sell
   - Manage or advise on portfolio allocation
   - Facilitate securities transactions
   - Provide tax advice
   - Act as a dealer or underwriter

### License Disclaimer

The MIT license already includes a warranty disclaimer, but the README and install script will additionally highlight that the tool is not intended for making investment decisions without professional advice.

## Alternatives Considered

### No Disclaimers

**Rejected**: Creates unacceptable legal risk. Even open-source tools can face regulatory scrutiny if they appear to provide investment advice.

### Restrict to Educational Use Only

**Rejected**: Overly limiting. The tool is genuinely useful for VC professionals who already have the expertise to contextualize AI-generated analysis. Disclaimers appropriately scope the tool's role.

### Require Professional Certification

**Rejected**: Would eliminate most of the target audience. The tool augments professional judgment, it doesn't replace it.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Clear legal positioning reduces regulatory risk
- Transparent about tool limitations builds user trust
- Jurisdiction-specific language demonstrates awareness of local regulations
- Consistent disclaimer framework across all outputs

### Negative

- Disclaimers add visual noise to every output
- May create false impression that the tool is less capable than it is
- Cannot fully eliminate regulatory risk (only professional legal review can)
- Need to keep disclaimer language current with regulatory changes

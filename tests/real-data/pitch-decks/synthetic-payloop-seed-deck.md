## Slide 2: Problem
- 2.8M Latin American contractors work for US/EU companies — growing 34% annually
- Paying LatAm contractors is a nightmare: 3-7 day settlement, 4-9% FX fees, no tax compliance
- Companies risk misclassification penalties ($50K+ per worker in some jurisdictions)
- Contractors lose 6-12% of earnings to intermediaries and poor FX rates
- Existing solutions (Deel, Remote) charge $49-99/contractor/mo and focus on full EOR, overkill for many use cases

---

## Slide 3: Solution
**PayLoop** handles the entire contractor payment flow for LatAm:
- Instant contractor onboarding with local compliance (tax ID verification, contract generation)
- Same-day payouts in local currency via local banking rails (not SWIFT)
- FX at mid-market + 0.8% spread (vs. 3-5% industry average)
- Automated local tax withholding and year-end reporting for 7 countries
- Self-serve dashboard — no need to email support for every payment run

---

## Slide 4: Why Now
- US companies hired 430,000 new LatAm contractors in 2025 alone (Deel market report)
- Brazil, Mexico, Colombia each enacted new digital worker regulations in 2024-2025 — compliance is suddenly mandatory, not optional
- Real-time payment rails (PIX in Brazil, SPEI in Mexico, PSE in Colombia) enable same-day settlement for the first time
- Fintech licensing has gotten faster — we obtained our Mexico IFPE license in 9 months (previously 18+)

## Slide 13: Risk Factors
- **Regulatory gray area**: We operate under temporary/sandbox frameworks in 3 of 7 countries. Regulators could change rules. Mitigation: Roberto Castillo (Head of Compliance) has direct relationships with regulators in all 7 countries; we're actively seeking full licenses
- **Founder history**: Diego's previous startup (RemitX) was acqui-hired after nearly running out of money. Lesson learned: we maintain strict burn discipline and raise earlier. Current burn multiple: 2.8x
- **FX risk**: We hold LatAm currencies for up to 48 hours during settlement. Mitigation: hedging via forward contracts for MXN and BRL; daily settlement for volatile currencies (ARS)
- **Country concentration**: Mexico is 42% of volume. Diversification underway — Brazil growing fastest (was 18% in Q1, now 28%)
- **Take rate compression**: As we scale, larger clients will negotiate lower rates. We expect blended take rate to decline to 2.8% at $50M+ monthly volume

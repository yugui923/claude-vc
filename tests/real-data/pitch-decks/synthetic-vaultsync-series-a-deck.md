## Slide 2: Problem
- $3.4T in M&A transactions close annually — every one requires a data room
- Current solutions (Intralinks, Datasite, Firmex) are legacy platforms built in the 2000s — clunky, slow, and insecure
- 23% of M&A deals experience a data breach or unauthorized access during due diligence (Deloitte M&A Security Report 2025)
- Average data room costs $15,000-50,000 per deal and takes 2 weeks to set up
- Document watermarking and access controls are easily circumvented — screenshots, screen recording, copy-paste
- Law firms manage 30-80 active data rooms simultaneously — admin overhead is enormous

---

## Slide 3: Solution
**VaultSync** is a zero-trust data room platform designed for M&A due diligence:
- **Zero-Trust Architecture**: Every document access requires real-time authentication and authorization — no cached sessions, no persistent downloads
- **AI Document Prep**: Upload raw documents, AI automatically organizes into standard due diligence categories (financial, legal, IP, employment, etc.)
- **Forensic Watermarking**: Invisible, unique watermarks per viewer per session — traceable even from screenshots or photos of screens
- **Smart Redaction**: AI-powered PII and sensitive data redaction with human review workflow
- **Deal Analytics**: Real-time dashboard showing exactly who viewed what, for how long, and what they downloaded
- **Instant Setup**: Go from document upload to live data room in 4 hours (vs. 2 weeks industry average)

## Slide 6: Product
- **VaultSync Rooms**: Create deal-specific data rooms with granular permissions (view, download, print, copy — each controlled separately per user per folder)
- **AI Organizer**: Drag-and-drop 10,000 documents — AI categorizes them into the standard due diligence index in 20 minutes
- **Forensic Watermarking**: Every rendered page has an invisible, unique forensic watermark tied to the viewer's identity and timestamp. Survives screenshots, photos, and printing
- **Smart Redaction**: AI identifies and redacts SSNs, bank accounts, compensation data, trade secrets across 40+ document types. 97.2% recall, 94.8% precision
- **Q&A Workflow**: Built-in question-and-answer system for buyer/seller communication, with automatic privilege tagging
- **Deal Analytics**: Time-on-page analytics, document comparison heat maps, and activity alerts. Sellers can see buyer interest signals in real-time
- **Integrations**: NetDocuments, iManage, SharePoint, Box, DocuSign, and all major e-signature platforms

## Slide 13: Strategic Considerations
- **Why law firms first**: Law firms are the natural entry point — they manage data rooms for every deal. Once a firm adopts VaultSync, they bring us into every transaction. This is a distribution advantage, not a limitation
- **Vertical concentration risk (92% law firms)**: We plan to expand to PE firms, investment banks, and corporate development teams. But we're honest: the sales motion is different. Law firms buy through relationship-driven sales; corporates buy through procurement. We need to build that muscle
- **97-day sales cycle**: Long by SaaS standards but short for legal tech (industry average: 140 days). We're investing in a self-serve pilot program to let firms try VaultSync on a single deal before committing firm-wide
- **Expansion within accounts**: Our 156% NRR shows the land-and-expand playbook works. Typical pattern: litigation practice group adopts first, then M&A, then restructuring, then real estate
- **Regulatory tailwind**: EU DORA and NIST 2.0 zero-trust requirements are creating a compliance forcing function. Several prospects have told us their current data room provider won't pass their next security audit
- **Platform risk**: We rely on AWS for infrastructure with a secondary GCP deployment. No single-cloud dependency

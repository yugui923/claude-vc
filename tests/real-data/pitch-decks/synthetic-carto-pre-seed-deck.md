## Slide 2: Problem
- 168M Americans participate in outdoor recreation annually, spending $862B (BEA Outdoor Recreation Satellite Account)
- Yet there's no social platform built for them. They use Instagram (not activity-focused), AllTrails (no social), Strava (fitness tracking, not discovery), and group texts
- Finding people to hike, climb, kayak, or camp with is surprisingly hard — 47% of outdoor enthusiasts say they'd do more activities if they had partners (REI survey 2025)
- Trail conditions, gear beta, and local knowledge live in fragmented Facebook groups and Reddit threads
- Existing outdoor apps are tools (maps, tracking) — not communities

---

## Slide 3: Solution
**Carto** is a location-based social network designed around outdoor activities:
- **Activity Feed**: See what friends and locals are doing nearby — hikes, climbs, paddles, bike rides
- **Crew Finder**: Post an activity plan, get matched with compatible partners by skill level, pace, and availability
- **Trail Intel**: Crowdsourced real-time conditions (snow, mud, closures, water levels) with photo verification
- **Adventure Log**: GPS-tracked activity logs with photos, route data, and personal notes — your outdoor journal
- **Gear Swap**: Peer-to-peer marketplace for used outdoor gear (launching Q3 2026)

---

## Slide 4: Why Now
- Outdoor participation surged 12% post-COVID and has stayed elevated — it's structural, not a fad
- Gen Z over-indexes on outdoor activities (+23% vs. older cohorts) and wants social features native to the activity
- AllTrails' $150M raise and 50M+ users proved the outdoor digital market is massive — but they stopped at maps
- Location services + social graph technology is mature enough for privacy-respectful activity matching
- Creator economy hasn't reached outdoor recreation — outdoor influencers are stuck on Instagram with no activity-specific tools

## Slide 6: Product
- **Home Feed**: Activity posts, trail updates, and crew invitations from your network and nearby users
- **Crew Finder**: AI-matched activity partners. Set your skill level (beginner to expert), activity type, dates, and max group size. Algorithm matches based on 12 compatibility signals including pace, experience, and mutual connections
- **Trail Intel**: Real-time condition reports with recency scoring. Reports decay in relevance over 7 days. Photo verification badges for high-trust reports
- **Adventure Log**: Auto-tracked routes with GPS, elevation, photos, and trip notes. Shareable cards for social media
- **Explore Map**: Discover trails, crags, put-ins, and campsites near you with real-time condition overlays
- Supported activities: hiking, rock climbing, kayaking/canoeing, mountain biking, backpacking, trail running, skiing/snowboarding, fishing

## Slide 8: Revenue Strategy
**Currently $0 revenue — pre-monetization by design.** Prioritizing growth and engagement.

Planned revenue streams (launching H2 2026):
- **Carto Pro**: $7.99/mo — Offline maps, advanced route planning, unlimited Adventure Log storage, priority Crew Finder matching. Target: 8-12% conversion of MAU
- **Gear Swap marketplace**: 10% transaction fee on peer-to-peer gear sales. AllTrails shows outdoor users will pay for premium; REI's used gear program shows they'll buy/sell used equipment
- **Sponsored Trails**: Outdoor brands (REI, Patagonia, The North Face) can sponsor trail pages and activity challenges. CPM model. Target launch: Q1 2027
- **Outfitter Partnerships**: Local guide services and gear rental shops listed with booking integration. 15% commission. Target launch: Q2 2027

Revenue projection: $0 in 2026 -> $840K in 2027 -> $4.2M in 2028 (conditional on hitting 500K MAU)

## Slide 9: Growth Strategy
- **Core loop**: Do activity -> Log it -> Share to social media -> Friends discover Carto -> Download -> Find crew -> Do activity
- **Phase 1 (now)**: Geographic density in 5 outdoor hubs (Boulder, Bay Area, Seattle, Portland, Asheville). Social products need density — we'd rather have 10,000 active users in Boulder than 50,000 spread across the country
- **Phase 2 (post-seed)**: Expand to 20 metros with strong outdoor cultures. Launch Ambassador program — recruit local outdoor leaders
- **Phase 3 (Series A)**: National expansion, international (BC Canada, Chamonix, Queenstown NZ)
- **Acquisition channels**: Instagram/TikTok organic content (Adventure Log shares), partnership with local outdoor retailers, climbing gym and trail race event sponsorships, SEO on trail condition content
- **Current CAC**: $0.82 (organic-heavy; 78% of users come from friend invites or social shares)

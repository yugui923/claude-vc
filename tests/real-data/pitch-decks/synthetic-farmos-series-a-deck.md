## Slide 2: Problem
- Precision agriculture promises 15-25% yield improvements, but 88% of farms under 2,000 acres can't afford it
- John Deere's precision stack costs $180K+ per tractor — designed for mega-farms, out of reach for mid-size operations

- Mid-size farms (200-2,000 acres) produce 38% of US crop value but receive <5% of precision ag investment

## Slide 3: Solution
**FarmOS** provides affordable, sensor-backed precision agriculture for mid-size farms:
- **FarmOS Pods**: Low-cost wireless sensor clusters ($280/unit) measuring soil moisture, temperature, nitrogen, phosphorus, and pH at 3 depths
- **FarmOS Dashboard**: Real-time field-level insights — irrigation scheduling, fertilizer recommendations, pest risk alerts
- **FarmOS Forecast**: Hyperlocal weather and microclimate predictions using sensor data + satellite imagery + NOAA models
- **FarmOS Advisor**: AI agronomist — upload a photo of crop stress, get diagnosis and treatment recommendations
- **FarmOS Marketplace**: Input purchasing at group-discount pricing (seed, fertilizer, crop protection)

## Slide 6: Product Details
- **FarmOS Pod**: Solar-powered sensor cluster, 3-year battery life, LoRaWAN + satellite backup connectivity. Measures: soil moisture (3 depths), soil temperature, nitrogen, phosphorus, pH, ambient temperature, humidity, wind speed. Recommended density: 1 pod per 40 acres
- **FarmOS Gateway**: Hub that aggregates pod data and transmits via cellular. 1 gateway covers 200+ pods (8,000+ acres)
- **Dashboard**: Field maps with overlay layers (moisture, nutrients, yield prediction). Irrigation scheduling engine saves average of 18% water usage. Variable-rate fertilizer recommendations
- **FarmOS Advisor**: Crop disease identification from smartphone photos — 93.4% accuracy across 47 common diseases for corn, soybean, wheat, and cotton
- **Seasonal Reports**: End-of-season analytics showing ROI per field, input cost vs. yield correlation, and multi-year trend analysis

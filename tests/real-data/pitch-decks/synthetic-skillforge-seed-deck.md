## Slide 2: Problem
- 3.4M unfilled trade jobs in the US — electricians, plumbers, HVAC techs, welders (BLS 2025)
- Trade workers earn $52K-95K/yr but there's no Duolingo or Coursera for them
- Community college trade programs have 18-month wait lists and 44% dropout rates
- Existing online learning (Udemy, YouTube) is passive video — no hands-on practice assessment
- Average apprentice gets 3.2 hours/week of mentor time — not enough for mastery
- The skilled trades workforce is aging out: 53% of licensed electricians are over 50

---

## Slide 3: Solution
**SkillForge** is an AI tutoring platform purpose-built for trade skills:
- **AR-Guided Practice**: Point your phone at your work — AI gives real-time feedback on technique (e.g., pipe joint angle, wire gauge selection, duct sizing)
- **Adaptive Curriculum**: AI adjusts difficulty based on performance, not time spent
- **Code Compliance Engine**: Every exercise teaches to current local building codes (NEC, UPC, IMC) — updated automatically
- **Mentor Matching**: Connect with licensed professionals for 1-on-1 video sessions when AI can't answer
- **Certification Prep**: Practice tests mapped to state licensing exams with 89% first-attempt pass rate (vs. 62% national average)

## Slide 13: Addressing the Churn Elephant
We know 8% monthly churn is high. Here's our honest analysis:
- **52% of churn is graduation**: Students complete their certification target and leave. This is success, not failure
- **23% is price sensitivity**: $45/mo is a stretch for some apprentices earning $15-20/hr. We're testing a $25/mo lite tier
- **15% is content gaps**: Students hit the end of available content in their track. Adding welding and carpentry will help
- **10% is feature/quality**: AR assessment not available for their specific task, or app bugs
- **Our strategy to reduce churn**: (1) Multi-trade bundles to capture graduation churn into next skill, (2) Employer-paid subscriptions where churn is 3.1% monthly, (3) Certification maintenance/continuing education content for licensed professionals
- **Long-term view**: We believe the trade skills market is more like fitness (ongoing) than test prep (one-time). Our most engaged users practice 4-5x/week for ongoing skill maintenance. We need to build the retention loops to prove this thesis

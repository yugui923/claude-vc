## Non-Standard Provisions Flagged

### HIGH IMPACT: Board Composition Gives Investors De Facto Control

**What it says:** Board of 5 — two seats for Acme Ventures, two for common holders, one independent mutually agreed.

**Why it matters:** Founders do not hold a board majority. The "mutually agreed" independent seat effectively becomes a swing vote. If Acme influences the independent director selection, they can achieve 3-2 control. At Series A, founders typically retain board control (e.g., 2 founders + 1 investor + 1 independent, or 2-1 with a 3-seat board).

**Market context:** Investor-parity or investor-majority boards are uncommon before Series B/C. This structure is more typical of a Series B or a round where the company had limited leverage.

**Negotiation suggestion:** Propose a 5-seat board with 2 founder seats, 1 investor seat, and 2 independents mutually agreed — or a simpler 3-seat board (2 common, 1 preferred) with expansion rights at Series B.

---

### MEDIUM IMPACT: Restrictive $500K Debt Threshold

**What it says:** Protective provision #7 requires Series A consent for any indebtedness exceeding $500,000.

**Why it matters:** A $500K cap can constrain routine business operations — equipment financing, a small line of credit, or even vendor payment terms. For a company that just raised $15M, this threshold is disproportionately low and forces the company to seek investor approval for modest financial operations.

**Market context:** Standard debt consent thresholds at Series A range from $1M to $2M. The $500K figure is more typical of a seed round.

**Negotiation suggestion:** Increase to $1M-$1.5M, or carve out specific categories (equipment leases, standard vendor terms, credit facilities under $1M).

---

### MEDIUM IMPACT: 8% Dividend Rate Above Market

**What it says:** Non-cumulative dividends of 8% per annum, payable when and if declared by the Board, with participation alongside common on an as-converted basis.

**Why it matters:** While non-cumulative (so it doesn't accrue), the 8% rate is above the typical 6% benchmark. If the board ever declares dividends, preferred holders receive 8% of their investment ($1.2M/year) before common holders receive anything, then participate again on an as-converted basis. The "participation" language on dividends is also worth noting — it allows double-dipping on dividend payments.

**Market context:** Most Series A term sheets either omit dividends entirely or specify 6% non-cumulative. The practical impact is low since startups rarely declare dividends, but it sets a precedent for future rounds.

**Negotiation suggestion:** Reduce to 6% or remove the dividend provision entirely. If retained, remove the as-converted participation language so preferred holders choose between the 8% preference OR as-converted payout, not both.

---

### LOW-MEDIUM IMPACT: High Major Investor Threshold for Pro-Rata

**What it says:** Pro-rata rights limited to Major Investors holding at least 500,000 shares (~$2.4M invested).

**Why it matters:** This threshold excludes the Angel Syndicate ($2M total, split among various participants) from pro-rata rights in future rounds. Only Acme Ventures and TechFund Capital qualify. Smaller investors lose the ability to maintain their ownership percentage.

**Market context:** Major investor thresholds typically range from $250K to $1M at Series A.

**Negotiation suggestion:** Lower to $250K-$500K invested to include engaged angels, or set the threshold in dollar terms rather than share count.

## Summary

**Overall Assessment: Balanced, with investor-favorable board structure**

The term sheet is largely market-standard on economics (1x non-participating, broad-based weighted average anti-dilution) and founder terms (standard vesting, acceleration). The primary concern is the board composition, which denies founders clear control at Series A. Secondary issues — the low debt threshold, elevated dividend rate, and high pro-rata threshold — are negotiable but worth addressing.

**Top 3 Negotiation Priorities:**

1. **Board composition** — Restructure to give founders clear majority (2 common + 1 preferred + 2 independent, or reduce to 3 seats). This is the most consequential term in the sheet.
2. **Debt consent threshold** — Increase from $500K to $1M-$1.5M to avoid operational friction.
3. **Dividend rate and participation** — Reduce to 6% or eliminate; remove as-converted participation on dividends.

**Terms That Are Standard (no action needed):**
- 1x non-participating liquidation preference
- Broad-based weighted average anti-dilution
- 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff
- Single-trigger 25% + double-trigger 100% acceleration
- ROFR / co-sale rights
- Registration rights
- 45-day no-shop
- $40K legal fee cap

**Missing Terms to Consider Adding:**
- **Pay-to-play provision** — Protects founders by ensuring existing investors participate in future rounds or lose preferred status
- **Non-compete/non-solicit clause** — Absence benefits founders, but investors may request this; be prepared to negotiate scope and duration

**Founder-Favorable Highlight:**
- The **3x drag-along floor** is above the typical 2x threshold, providing founders meaningful protection against being forced into a low-value exit. This is a positive term worth preserving.

## Term Sheet Analysis: NVCA Model Term Sheet (2020)

**Document**: NVCA Model Term Sheet for Series A Preferred Stock Financing

---

### Overview

This is the **NVCA Model Term Sheet itself** — the industry-standard template from which most VC term sheets are drafted. It presents **multiple alternatives** for key provisions rather than selecting specific terms. The analysis below evaluates each alternative offered and flags which choices are standard, investor-favorable, or aggressive.

### Non-Standard Provisions Flagged

#### 1. Cumulative Dividends (Alternative 3) — **High Impact**

**What it says**: "The Series A Preferred will carry an annual [__]% cumulative dividend payable upon a liquidation or redemption."

**Why it matters**: Cumulative dividends accrue whether declared or not, effectively increasing the liquidation preference over time. At 8% cumulative over 5 years, a $10M investment becomes a ~$14.7M liquidation preference before common stockholders see a dollar.

**Market context**: Non-cumulative dividends are standard at Series A. Cumulative dividends are more commonly seen in growth equity or late-stage deals.

**Negotiation suggestion**: If an investor selects this alternative, push for Alternative 1 (as-converted when paid on common) or Alternative 2 (non-cumulative, board-declared). If cumulative is unavoidable, negotiate for payment only at a Deemed Liquidation Event, not redemption.

#### 2. Participating Preferred (Alternatives 2 & 3) — **High Impact**

**What it says**: Alt 2 gives investors their liquidation preference AND pro-rata participation in remaining proceeds. Alt 3 is the same but with a cap.

**Why it matters**: Participating preferred creates "double-dipping" — investors get their money back first, then share in the upside alongside common. In a modest exit (e.g., 3x return), this can significantly reduce founder and employee proceeds versus non-participating.

**Market context**: 1x non-participating (Alternative 1) is market standard for Series A. Participating preferred is more common in later rounds or in down markets. Alt 3 (capped participation) is a reasonable middle ground if participation is required.

**Negotiation suggestion**: Push for Alternative 1 (non-participating). If the investor insists on participation, negotiate for a cap (Alternative 3) at 2-3x the Original Purchase Price.

#### 3. Redemption Rights — **High Impact**

**What it says**: "The Series A Preferred shall be redeemable at the option of the Requisite Holders commencing any time after the five (5) year anniversary of the Closing."

**Why it matters**: Redemption forces the company to buy back shares with cash, which most startups don't have. This creates leverage for investors to force a sale or refinancing. While rarely exercised in practice, it's a significant overhang.

**Market context**: Redemption rights have become **less common** in recent years, especially at Series A. Many top-tier VCs do not require them. The NVCA includes them as optional (bracketed).

**Negotiation suggestion**: Push to remove entirely. If investor insists, extend the trigger to 7 years, add a board approval requirement, and ensure redemption is subordinate to all debt.

#### 4. Acceleration on Change of Control — **Missing**

**What it says**: The term sheet does not address single-trigger or double-trigger acceleration for founders upon a change of control.

**Why it matters**: Without acceleration, founders and key employees could lose unvested equity in an acquisition, reducing alignment and retention.

**Market context**: Single-trigger partial acceleration (25-50% of unvested) or double-trigger full acceleration is increasingly standard at Series A.

**Negotiation suggestion**: Add a provision for at minimum double-trigger full acceleration (termination within 12 months of CIC) for founders and C-suite.

#### 5. Protective Provisions — Broad Scope — **Medium Impact**

**What it says**: Provision (vi) requires preferred consent to "adopt, amend, terminate or repeal any equity compensation plan or amend or waive any of the terms of any option or other grant."

**Why it matters**: This gives investors veto power over individual option grants and plan amendments, which can slow down hiring and retention efforts. Standard protective provisions cover plan adoption but not individual grants.

**Market context**: Standard practice is board-level approval (with preferred director participation) for option plans, not a full class vote on individual grants.

**Negotiation suggestion**: Limit preferred class consent to adoption/termination of equity plans and aggregate pool increases. Individual grants should require only board approval (including preferred director vote).

#### 6. Preferred Director Approval Matters — **Medium Impact**

**What it says**: Extensive list of matters requiring preferred director approval including hiring/firing executives, compensation changes, and any indebtedness over a threshold.

**Why it matters**: While board-level governance is reasonable, requiring specific preferred director approval (vs. majority board vote) for operational matters like executive hiring gives disproportionate control to a single board seat.

**Market context**: These provisions are included in the NVCA model but are optional (bracketed). Many Series A deals rely on standard board majority vote rather than specific preferred director veto rights.

**Negotiation suggestion**: Limit preferred director approval rights to high-impact items (debt, IP licensing, subsidiary creation). Operational decisions (hiring, compensation) should require only board majority.

---

### Summary

**Overall Assessment**: **Balanced (as a template)** — The NVCA Model Term Sheet is designed as a menu of options. When the standard alternatives are selected (Alt 1 for dividends, Alt 1 for liquidation preference, no redemption), the result is a balanced, founder-fair deal. When the aggressive alternatives are selected, the deal becomes significantly investor-favorable.

**Top 3 Negotiation Priorities** (when using this template):

1. **Liquidation preference** — Insist on Alternative 1 (1x non-participating). This is the single highest-impact economic term. Participating preferred at Series A is a red flag
2. **Redemption rights** — Push to remove entirely. They are optional in this template and increasingly uncommon at early stages
3. **Acceleration on CIC** — Add founder-protective acceleration language. Its absence is a notable gap in the template

**Terms That Are Standard**:
- Broad-based weighted average anti-dilution
- 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff
- Pro-rata rights with over-subscription
- ROFR and co-sale
- 180-day IPO lock-up
- Drag-along with majority common + preferred vote
- Standard protective provisions (core set)
- No-shop / confidentiality

**Missing Terms to Consider Adding**:
- Single/double-trigger acceleration on change of control
- Founder-friendly carve-outs on non-compete (especially if in CA, CO, MN)
- SAFE/note conversion mechanics (if applicable — referenced but not detailed)
- Specific option pool size and impact on pre-money valuation

## Detailed Analysis

### Growth Metrics

| Metric | Q4 FY25 | Full Year FY25 | Trend |
|--------|---------|----------------|-------|
| Total Revenue Growth (YoY) | 25% | 29% | Slight deceleration Q4 vs. FY |
| Subscription Revenue Growth (YoY) | 27% | 31% | Subscription outpacing total |
| ARR Growth (YoY) | 23% | — | Healthy at scale |
| Q4 Net New ARR | $224.3M | — | Solid quarterly addition |

**Assessment**: CrowdStrike is sustaining ~25-30% growth at $4B+ ARR scale, which is exceptional. The slight Q4 deceleration (25% vs. 29% full-year) is natural at this scale. Subscription revenue growing faster than total revenue (31% vs. 29%) signals improving revenue quality.

**FY26 Guidance**: $4.744-$4.806B revenue implies ~20-22% growth — a controlled deceleration consistent with scale.

### Unit Economics

| Metric | Value | Assessment |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Gross Retention | 97% | Elite-tier retention; only 3% annual logo churn |
| Module Adoption (5+) | 67% | Strong platform stickiness |
| Module Adoption (8+) | 21% | Significant cross-sell runway remains |
| Emerging Products ARR | $1.3B+ | ~31% of total ARR from newer products |

**Assessment**: The 97% gross retention signals extremely low involuntary and voluntary churn, among the best in enterprise SaaS. Module adoption data (67% at 5+ modules) demonstrates strong platform consolidation. The $1.3B+ in Next-Gen SIEM, Cloud Security, and Identity Protection shows successful expansion beyond core endpoint.

**NRR (estimated)**: Not explicitly reported, but with 97% gross retention and 23% ARR growth at this scale, implied NRR is likely **115-120%+**, indicating healthy expansion revenue from the existing base.

**Burn Multiple**: Not applicable — the company is FCF positive. It generated $1.07B in free cash flow while adding ~$793M in net new ARR, effectively a **negative burn multiple** (generating $1.35 in FCF per $1 of net new ARR). This is best-in-class capital efficiency.

## Flags

### Healthy
- **Rule of 40 at 56**: Exceptional balance of growth and profitability — top-decile among public SaaS
- **97% gross retention**: Near-zero involuntary churn indicates mission-critical product
- **27% FCF margin**: Generating over $1B in free cash flow while growing 29%
- **95% subscription mix**: Highly predictable, recurring revenue base
- **80% subscription gross margin**: Premium SaaS margins sustained at scale
- **$4.3B cash**: Fortress balance sheet provides strategic flexibility

### Watch
- **ARR growth decelerating**: 23% Q4 YoY vs. higher prior periods — natural at $4B+ but worth tracking against FY26 guidance
- **GAAP profitability**: Still GAAP net-loss (-$19.3M), though the gap is closing rapidly
- **SBC magnitude**: ~$958M+ in stock-based compensation creates significant GAAP/Non-GAAP divergence and real shareholder dilution
- **Professional services declining share**: $50M Q4 (~4.7% of revenue) — not a concern per se, but monitor for customer implementation support adequacy

## Recommendation

CrowdStrike presents an **elite SaaS financial profile** at scale. The combination of 29% revenue growth, 27% FCF margins, 97% gross retention, and 80% subscription gross margins places it among the highest-quality SaaS businesses globally. The Rule of 40 score of 56 and $1B+ FCF generation demonstrate that growth and profitability are not in tension.

**Key areas to monitor:**
1. **ARR growth trajectory** — FY26 guidance implies ~20-22% growth; watch for stabilization vs. further deceleration
2. **NRR disclosure** — the company hasn't explicitly reported NRR in this release; request this metric for a complete unit economics picture
3. **Module adoption** — the 21% at 8+ modules represents significant remaining cross-sell opportunity
4. **GAAP convergence** — at -$19.3M net loss, the company is quarters away from sustained GAAP profitability

From a valuation perspective, at 29% growth with 80% gross margins, CrowdStrike commands premium SaaS multiples (high-end of EV/ARR 6-30x range per industry benchmarks). The strong FCF generation increasingly supports EBITDA-based valuation as well.

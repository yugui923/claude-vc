### Conversion Mechanics (Sec. 1(a))

On Equity Financing, SAFE converts to the **greater** of:
1. Purchase Amount / lowest price per share of Standard Preferred Stock (round price)
2. Purchase Amount / Safe Price, where **Safe Price = Post-Money Valuation Cap / Company Capitalization**

This gives the investor the better of the round price or the capped price.

### Liquidity Event (Sec. 1(b))

Investor receives the **greater** of:
- **Cash-Out Amount**: Return of Purchase Amount (1x)
- **Conversion Amount**: As-converted share of proceeds (Purchase Amount / Liquidity Price)

### Dissolution Event (Sec. 1(c))

Investor receives the **Cash-Out Amount** only (1x return of investment).

### Liquidation Priority (Sec. 1(d))

| Priority | Claim |
|----------|-------|
| Senior | Debt, creditor claims, unconverted notes |
| Pari passu | Other SAFEs and Preferred Stock |
| Junior | Common Stock |

### Control & Governance

- **Voting rights**: None until conversion (Sec. 5(c))
- **Board seat**: None
- **Information rights**: None specified
- **Pro-rata rights**: Not included in this variant

### Amendment (Sec. 5(a))

Modifiable by Company + Investor consent, **or** by Company + majority-in-interest of SAFEs with the same cap/discount terms. Purchase Amount cannot be amended by majority vote.

### Transferability (Sec. 5(d))

Not transferable without consent, except to: estate/heirs (death/disability) or affiliates/controlled entities.

### Tax Treatment (Sec. 5(g))

Treated as common stock for U.S. federal and state income tax purposes (Sections 304, 305, 306, 354, 368, 1036, 1202 IRC).

## Non-Standard Provisions Flagged

### 1. No Pro-Rata Rights — **Medium Impact**

**What it says**: This SAFE does not include a side letter or provision granting the investor the right to participate in the next priced round.

**Why it matters**: Without pro-rata rights, the investor cannot maintain their ownership percentage when a Series A occurs. For investors writing checks >$250K, this is typically expected.

**Market context**: The standard YC post-money SAFE template intentionally omits pro-rata — YC provides it via a separate side letter (the "Pro Rata Side Letter"). This is a template design choice, not a red flag.

**Action**: If the investor wants pro-rata rights, negotiate a separate YC Pro Rata Side Letter. This is common practice and does not require modifying the SAFE itself.

### 2. No Discount Rate — **Low Impact**

**What it says**: This is the cap-only variant; there is no discount applied to the priced round price.

**Why it matters**: In the pre-money SAFE era, discounts (15-25%) were common alongside or instead of caps. Post-money SAFEs typically use cap-only, making this standard.

**Assessment**: This is the intended design of this YC variant. No action needed unless the investor specifically wants a discount, in which case the "Valuation Cap and Discount" variant should be used instead.

## Overall Assessment

**Balanced (Standard YC Form)**

This is the unmodified YC Post-Money SAFE (Valuation Cap Only) — the most widely used SAFE template in early-stage venture. The document explicitly states at line 6 that neither party has modified the form. All provisions match the standard YC template.

### Top 3 Negotiation Points

1. **Valuation cap amount** — The single most important economic term to negotiate. Compare against current market valuations for the company's stage, traction, and sector
2. **Pro-rata side letter** — If the investor wants the right to maintain ownership in future rounds, negotiate a separate YC Pro Rata Side Letter alongside this SAFE
3. **Purchase amount** — Size the investment relative to the cap to understand implied ownership (ownership % = purchase amount / valuation cap)

### Terms That Are Standard (Reassurance)
- Post-money structure with clear ownership math
- 1x non-participating liquidation preference equivalent
- Greater-of conversion (investor gets better of round price or cap price)
- Standard liquidation priority waterfall
- Standard company representations
- Standard accredited investor requirement
- Tax treatment as common stock (QSBS eligibility preserved)
- Amendment requires majority-in-interest (can't be changed without broad consent)

### Missing Terms to Add (If Desired)
- **Pro-rata side letter** — standard companion document for institutional investors
- **Information rights side letter** — if investor wants quarterly/annual financials
- **Board observer rights** — not typical at SAFE stage, but sometimes requested by lead investors

### Analysis

**Profitability**: CrowdStrike is already profitable on a non-GAAP basis. The model shows net income of $746M in FY2026 growing to ~$2B by FY2030, consistent with their operating leverage trajectory.

**Margin Progression**: EBITDA margins expand from 21% to 31% over 5 years — driven by gross margin improvement (80% → 82%) and operating leverage as S&M and G&A scale sub-linearly. This is consistent with best-in-class cybersecurity SaaS at scale.

**Cash Generation**: Operating cash flow grows from $187M (depressed by Y1 working capital build on $720M initial AR) to $2.0B by FY2030. Ending cash reaches $7.9B with no additional financing — the company is self-funding.

**Key Sensitivities**:
- **Growth rate** is the largest lever — each 1% change in revenue growth compounds to ~$400M in terminal revenue
- **Gross margin** stability at 80%+ is critical; any degradation from competitive pricing pressure directly hits EBITDA
- **S&M efficiency** determines how fast operating leverage materializes — the 97% gross retention rate supports the assumed S&M decline as % of revenue
- **CapEx intensity** at 8% is high for pure SaaS — cloud infrastructure and capitalized R&D are the main drivers; any reduction flows directly to FCF

**Validation vs. Guidance**: FY2026 modeled revenue of $4.78B falls within company guidance of $4.74–4.81B. Modeled non-GAAP operating income of ~$1.0B aligns with guided $944–985M (model is slightly higher due to simplified cost structure).

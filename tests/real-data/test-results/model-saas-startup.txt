### Analysis

**Break-even timing**: The company reaches **EBITDA profitability in Year 3 (2028)** and net income profitability in the same year — consistent with the deck's projections. EBITDA margin expands from -5% to +13% by Year 5 as operating leverage kicks in.

**Cash runway**: With the $15M Series A, cash peaks at $16.5M (Year 1) and troughs at $10.6M (Year 3) before inflecting upward. The company **never runs out of cash** and ends Year 5 with $21.7M — no additional fundraise required if the plan holds. However, at the Year 2-3 burn rate (~$3M/yr net cash drain), a delayed breakeven by even 1 year would push the trough to ~$7.7M, still manageable but tight.

**Margin progression**: Gross margins expand modestly (82% → 84%) as infrastructure costs scale sub-linearly. The real story is EBITDA margin expansion from -5% to +13%, driven by S&M efficiency (45% → 37% of revenue) and G&A leverage (12% → 9%). This is a textbook SaaS operating leverage pattern.

**Key sensitivities**:
- **Growth rate is the dominant driver**: A 10-point reduction in Year 2-3 growth rates would push breakeven to Year 4 and reduce Year 5 revenue by ~$30M
- **S&M efficiency**: If S&M stays flat at 45% instead of declining, breakeven pushes to Year 5 and cash runs dangerously low (~$3M)
- **NRR of 135% provides a cushion**: High expansion revenue reduces required new logo acquisition, supporting the growth trajectory
- **Working capital**: AR is the largest non-cash balance sheet item ($18.9M by Year 5) — collection efficiency matters as ACV scales with enterprise customers

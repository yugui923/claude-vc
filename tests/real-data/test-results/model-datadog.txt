### Analysis

**Profitability**: Datadog is already profitable on a non-GAAP basis. The model shows EBITDA margins expanding from 23% to 33% over 5 years as operating leverage kicks in — consistent with the trajectory of scaled SaaS companies like CrowdStrike and ServiceNow.

**Cash generation**: Year 1 operating CF appears lower ($402M) because of the large initial AR build from a zero base in the model. Normalizing for that, steady-state OCF grows from ~$1.0B (Year 2) to ~$2.0B (Year 5). This aligns with Datadog's current run-rate of ~$830M/year annualized FCF (9-month figure: $624M).

**Cash position**: Starting from $4.1B in liquid assets, cash grows to $9.7B by Year 5 with no acquisitions or buybacks — significant capital allocation optionality. In practice, Datadog will likely deploy capital toward M&A (AI observability, security) and share repurchases.

**Margin progression**: Gross margin improves modestly (75% → 77%) reflecting infrastructure optimization. The real story is operating leverage: total OpEx as a % of revenue drops from ~52% to ~44%, driving EBITDA margin expansion of ~200bps/year.

**Key sensitivities**:
- **Growth deceleration rate**: Each 2pp faster deceleration reduces Year 5 revenue by ~$600M and EBITDA by ~$200M
- **Gross margin**: Each 1pp change flows through ~$75M in Year 5 EBITDA
- **AI adoption**: Datadog's AI native customer cohort (12% of revenue, doubling YoY) could accelerate or sustain growth rates above model assumptions
- **Competition**: Pricing pressure from Grafana/open-source alternatives could compress margins below forecast

**Note**: This model excludes stock-based compensation (~$1B+ annually), which is the primary difference between Datadog's GAAP and non-GAAP results. GAAP operating margins would be materially lower.

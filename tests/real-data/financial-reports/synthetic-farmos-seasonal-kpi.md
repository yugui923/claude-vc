### Seasonal Revenue Distribution
| Quarter | Software Rev | Hardware Rev | Total Rev | % of Annual |

**Seasonality Note:** Revenue is driven by the agricultural calendar in North
American row crop farming. Q1 is pre-season (minimal activity). Q2 is planting
season (sensor deployments + new subscriptions). Q3 is growing season (peak
platform usage, in-season upsells, and sensor add-ons for crop scouting).
Q4 is harvest/post-harvest (subscription renewals happen, but hardware sales
drop as fields are cleared). Hardware revenue is almost zero in Q4 and Q1.

### Customer Metrics

### NRR Detail — Why Only 105%
- Farmers expand slowly — adding sensors to new fields is a physical deployment
- Typical expansion: 2-4 additional sensors per year ($180-$360 hardware + $120-$240 software)
- No usage-based pricing to drive natural expansion
- Considering pivot to per-acre pricing model to improve NRR (target: 115%)

### Unit Economics
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): $2,800 (blended)
  - Field sales (60% of new customers): $3,600 CAC
  - Dealer/co-op channel (30%): $1,800 CAC
  - Direct/inbound (10%): $900 CAC
- Average Customer Lifetime: 8.2 years (based on 96.8% annual retention)
- Lifetime Value (LTV): $31,400 (8.2 years x $6,053/year x 63.1% blended margin)
- LTV/CAC: 11.2x
- CAC Payback Period: 14 months (on contribution margin)
- Note: Long payback is acceptable given high retention — farmers who adopt rarely leave

### Key Risks
1. Extreme seasonality creates Q1 cash crunch every year — may need credit facility
2. Hardware margin at 28% drags blended margin below SaaS benchmarks
3. NRR of 105% limits organic growth — per-acre pricing could help but risks churn
4. Field sales model is expensive ($3,600 CAC) — need to scale dealer channel
5. Sensor failure rate of 8% drives warranty costs and customer frustration
6. Climate volatility (drought, flooding) can delay deployments and reduce farmer budgets
7. Competitor risk: John Deere and CNH Industrial building integrated solutions

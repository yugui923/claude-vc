# TERM SHEET
## Series B Preferred Stock Financing of MedScribe AI, Inc.

**Date:** November 3, 2025
**Confidential**

This Term Sheet summarizes the principal terms for the Series B Preferred Stock
Financing of MedScribe AI, Inc., a Delaware corporation (the "Company"). This
document is intended solely for discussion purposes and is non-binding except
for the "No-Shop," "Confidentiality," and "Legal Fees" provisions.

---

### CHARTER PROVISIONS

**Dividends:** Non-cumulative dividends at 6% per annum, payable when declared
by the Board. Series B participates pari passu with Series A and Common Stock
on an as-converted basis for any additional dividends declared.

**Liquidation Preference:** 1.0x non-participating preferred. In a Liquidation
Event, Series B Preferred receives the greater of (i) 1.0x Original Purchase
Price plus declared but unpaid dividends, or (ii) the as-converted amount.
Series B is senior to Series A, which is senior to Common Stock.

**Anti-Dilution:**
- **For General Atlantic's $25,000,000 allocation:** Full ratchet anti-dilution.
  If shares are issued below the Series B price, GA's conversion price is
  reduced to the new issuance price regardless of the number of shares issued.
- **For all other Series B holders (Tiger Global, Bessemer):** Broad-based
  weighted average anti-dilution protection.
- Standard carve-outs apply to both tiers: employee incentive grants (up to
  the approved pool), strategic issuances approved by the Board, and conversions
  of existing outstanding instruments.

**Conversion:** Series B Preferred is convertible at any time into Common Stock
at the applicable conversion ratio (initially 1:1), subject to anti-dilution
adjustments.

**Automatic Conversion:** Series B converts automatically upon (i) a firmly
underwritten IPO with gross proceeds of at least $100,000,000 and a price per
share of at least 2x the Series B Original Purchase Price, or (ii) consent of
holders of 66.7% of the outstanding Series B Preferred (voting as a single
class).

**Voting Rights:** Series B votes with all other classes on an as-converted
basis. Protective provisions require consent of holders of a majority of
Series B Preferred for:
1. Amendment of Charter or Bylaws adversely affecting Series B
2. Authorization of equity senior to or pari passu with Series B
3. Any merger, sale, or change of control
4. Redemption of any equity securities (other than at-cost employee repurchases)
5. Incurrence of debt exceeding $5,000,000
6. Capital expenditures exceeding $3,000,000 in any fiscal year
7. Entry into material agreements with founders, officers, or affiliates
8. Liquidation or dissolution

### BOARD OF DIRECTORS

**Composition:** The Board shall be expanded to seven (7) members:
- Two (2) designated by General Atlantic
- One (1) designated by Bessemer Venture Partners (continuing from Series A)
- Two (2) designated by the Common Stock holders (CEO and one founder)
- Two (2) independent members jointly agreed by GA and the CEO

**Board Observer:** Tiger Global shall have the right to designate one Board
observer who may attend all Board and committee meetings in a non-voting
capacity. The observer shall receive all Board materials on the same schedule
as directors. The Company may exclude the observer from discussions involving
matters in which Tiger Global has a direct conflict of interest.

**Board Committees:** An Audit Committee and Compensation Committee shall be
established, each with at least one independent director. General Atlantic
shall have a seat on each committee.

---

### INVESTOR RIGHTS

**Information Rights:** Monthly unaudited financial statements within 20 days
of month-end. Quarterly financial statements reviewed by an independent auditor
within 30 days of quarter-end. Annual audited financials (Big Four) within 75
days of fiscal year-end. Annual operating budget at least 30 days prior to each
fiscal year.

**Pro-Rata Rights:** Each Series B investor holding at least $5,000,000 of
Series B Preferred shall have pro-rata rights in future equity financings.

**Super Pro-Rata Rights:** General Atlantic shall have the right to purchase up
to 2.5x its pro rata share in any future equity financing of the Company,
exercisable within 20 business days of notice. This right is personal to
General Atlantic and non-transferable.

**Registration Rights:** Three demand registrations beginning 180 days after IPO.
Unlimited piggyback registrations. S-3 registration rights when eligible.
Company bears all expenses except underwriting discounts.

---

### ADDITIONAL TERMS

**Right of First Refusal / Co-Sale:** Company and then Series B investors have
ROFR on any secondary sale of shares by founders or employees holding more
than 1% of the Company. Tag-along rights for all investors on founder sales.

**Drag-Along:** Holders of 60% of all outstanding shares (on an as-converted
basis), including at least a majority of Series B, may compel all stockholders
to approve a Company sale.

**Founder Lock-Up on IPO:** Each founder agrees to a mandatory lock-up of
3 years from the date of an IPO, during which no founder may sell, transfer,
hedge, or otherwise dispose of any shares. This exceeds the standard 180-day
lock-up. After the 3-year period, founders may sell up to 25% of their holdings
per quarter. The lock-up may be waived by written consent of holders of 60% of
Series B Preferred.

**No-Shop / Exclusivity:** 60 days from the date of this Term Sheet. Company
may engage in discussions with existing investors regarding this round.

**Founder Vesting:** Existing unvested founder shares continue on current
schedule. No re-vesting required. Standard double-trigger acceleration: upon
(i) Change of Control plus (ii) involuntary termination within 12 months, 100%
of unvested shares vest.

**Key Person Provision:** If either Dr. Priya Sharma (CEO) or David Kim (CTO)
ceases to be a full-time employee within the first 24 months after Closing, the
holders of a majority of Series B Preferred shall have the right to appoint a
replacement CEO or CTO (as applicable) until the Board acts.

**Employee Matters:**
- All employees to sign CIIAs
- Standard 90-day post-termination exercise period
- Existing Series A employee terms unmodified

**Expenses:** Company to pay legal fees of General Atlantic (capped at $65,000)
and Tiger Global (capped at $35,000), totaling a maximum of $100,000.

**Closing Conditions:**
- Completion of legal, financial, commercial, and IP due diligence
- Receipt of FDA regulatory opinion letter regarding Company's AI documentation
  platform (Class I exempt status confirmation)
- Key person insurance of $10,000,000 on CEO and CTO
- Board-approved 2026 operating plan
- Execution of IRA, Voting Agreement, and ROFR/Co-Sale Agreement

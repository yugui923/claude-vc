# TERM SHEET
## Series A Preferred Stock Financing of NovaByte, Inc.

**Date:** September 8, 2025
**Confidential**

This Term Sheet summarizes the principal terms of the Series A Preferred Stock
Financing of NovaByte, Inc., a Delaware corporation (the "Company"). Except for
the sections entitled "No-Shop," "Confidentiality," and "Expenses," this term
sheet is non-binding and does not constitute an obligation of either party.

---

**Closing Date:** On or before October 31, 2025

**Amount Raised:** $12,000,000

**Pre-Money Valuation:** $50,000,000 (fully diluted, including the option pool
increase described below)

### CHARTER PROVISIONS

**Dividends:** Cumulative dividends at 8% per annum, compounding annually,
payable upon a Liquidation Event or redemption. Holders also participate in
dividends declared on Common Stock on an as-converted basis.

**Liquidation Preference:** 1.5x participating preferred. In any Liquidation
Event, holders of Series A Preferred shall receive 1.5 times the Original
Purchase Price plus any accrued but unpaid dividends before any distribution to
Common Stock. After payment of the Liquidation Preference, Series A Preferred
shall participate pro rata with Common Stock on an as-converted basis until
holders have received an aggregate of 3.0x the Original Purchase Price (the
"Participation Cap"), after which Series A Preferred ceases to participate and
Common Stock receives all remaining proceeds.

**Anti-Dilution:** Full ratchet anti-dilution protection. If the Company issues
shares at a price below the then-effective conversion price, the conversion price
shall be reduced to the price of the new issuance, without regard to the number
of shares issued. Standard carve-outs for employee incentive grants, strategic
partnerships approved by the Board, and conversion of outstanding convertible
instruments.

**Conversion:** Each share of Series A Preferred is convertible at any time into
Common Stock at the then-effective conversion ratio (initially 1:1).

**Automatic Conversion:** Series A Preferred converts automatically upon (i) a
Qualified IPO with gross proceeds of at least $75,000,000 and a per-share price
of at least 3x the Original Purchase Price, or (ii) written consent of holders
of 70% of outstanding Series A Preferred.

**Voting Rights:** Series A Preferred votes with Common Stock on an as-converted
basis. Protective provisions require consent of holders of at least 60% of
outstanding Series A Preferred for:
1. Amending the Certificate of Incorporation or Bylaws in a manner adverse to Series A
2. Authorizing or issuing equity securities senior to or on parity with Series A
3. Any merger, acquisition, asset sale, or change of control
4. Any redemption or repurchase of equity (other than at-cost repurchases from terminated employees)
5. Declaration of any dividend
6. Increasing or decreasing authorized shares
7. Incurrence of indebtedness exceeding $250,000 in the aggregate
8. Any related-party transaction with a founder, officer, director, or 5%+ stockholder
9. Any change to the size or composition of the Board
10. Entry into any new line of business or material modification to existing business

**Redemption:** Beginning on the 5th anniversary of the Closing, holders of a
majority of the Series A Preferred may require the Company to redeem all
outstanding Series A Preferred in three equal annual installments at 1.5x the
Original Purchase Price plus all accrued and unpaid dividends.

### BOARD OF DIRECTORS

**Composition:** The Board shall consist of five (5) members:
- Two (2) designated by Benchmark Capital as lead investor
- One (1) who is the Company's Chief Executive Officer
- One (1) designated by the holders of Common Stock (may not be an employee)
- One (1) independent member mutually agreed upon by Benchmark and the CEO,
  provided that if the parties cannot agree within 60 days of Closing, Benchmark
  shall have the right to designate this seat

**Board Matters:** Each Board member shall receive D&O insurance with coverage
of at least $5,000,000. Board meetings shall occur no less than quarterly.
Benchmark shall have the right to appoint one Board observer in addition to its
two designated seats.

---

### INVESTOR RIGHTS

**Information Rights:** Quarterly unaudited financial statements within 30 days
of quarter-end. Annual audited financials (Big Four firm) within 90 days of
fiscal year-end. Monthly management dashboards including revenue, burn rate,
headcount, and pipeline metrics within 15 days of month-end. Annual budget and
operating plan at least 30 days before each fiscal year.

**Pro-Rata Rights:** Each Major Investor (holding at least $2,000,000 of
Series A Preferred) shall have the right to purchase its pro rata share of
subsequent equity issuances. Benchmark Capital shall additionally have a
super pro-rata right to purchase up to 2x its pro rata share.

**Registration Rights:** Two demand registrations beginning 4 years after
Closing or 180 days after IPO, whichever is earlier. Unlimited piggyback rights.
S-3 registration rights when eligible. Company bears all expenses except
underwriting discounts and selling commissions.

---

### ADDITIONAL TERMS

**Right of First Refusal / Co-Sale:** Company and then investors have ROFR on
any transfer of founder shares. Investors have tag-along rights on founder sales.

**Drag-Along:** Holders of 60% of Common Stock (on an as-converted basis), with
approval of Benchmark, may compel all stockholders to approve a sale of the
Company, provided the transaction values Series A Preferred at no less than
1.5x the Original Purchase Price.

**No-Shop / Exclusivity:** For a period of 90 days from execution of this Term
Sheet, the Company shall not directly or indirectly solicit, encourage, negotiate,
or enter into any agreement regarding an Alternative Transaction, defined as
any equity financing, debt financing exceeding $250,000, merger, acquisition,
or sale of material assets. The Company shall notify Benchmark within 24 hours
of any unsolicited inquiry regarding an Alternative Transaction.

**Non-Compete:** Each founder agrees that for 24 months following termination
of employment (whether voluntary or involuntary), they will not directly or
indirectly engage in, own, manage, or participate in any business that competes
with the Company's core markets (cloud data synchronization and ETL tooling)
within the United States. This obligation survives any termination regardless
of cause.

**Founder Vesting:** All founder shares shall be subject to 4-year reverse
vesting with a 1-year cliff from the Closing Date, with 25% vesting at the
cliff and the remainder monthly. Prior service is not credited.

**Employee Matters:**
- All employees and contractors must execute CIIAs
- Standard 90-day post-termination exercise period for options
- No single-trigger acceleration for any employee
- Double-trigger acceleration of 50% for C-suite officers only

**Expenses:** Company shall reimburse Benchmark's legal fees up to $75,000
and due diligence expenses up to $15,000, payable at Closing or upon
termination of negotiations.

**Closing Conditions:**
- Satisfactory completion of legal, financial, and technical due diligence
- Receipt of audited 2024 financial statements
- Key person life insurance of $5,000,000 on CEO and CTO
- Founder employment agreements with 12-month non-solicitation clauses
- Execution of an investor rights agreement, voting agreement, and ROFR/co-sale agreement
